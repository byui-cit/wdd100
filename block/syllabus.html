WDD 100 Syllabus

Introduction to Web Design and Development

Welcome to Web Design 100! This class serves as an introduction to Web design and development. The course is designed to introduce students to basics of Web technology, Web design and development principles. The course uses hands-on activities with students actually participating in simple Web design projects and programming. It is anticipated that students who complete this course will understand the fields of Web design and development and will have a good idea of whether or not they want to pursue Web design and development as a career.

Time Consumption

Remember, because this is a one-credit block course, you will spend approximately 4-6 hours each week doing the homework. Do not plan otherwise. The most important thing to remember is to pace yourself. Set aside time throughout the week to complete your assignments.

Text

There is no textbook for this course. However, because many of the readings and assignments are online, students are recommended to have a steady internet connection.

Course Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Understand what the World Wide Web is and some of its uses in business and society
  • Develop simple Web pages
  • Understand basic Web design principles
  • Understand basic Web development principles
  • Determine if Web design and development is a career they would like to pursue

Final Project Description

There is one major class project for this course and it consists of two primary requirements. The first is the design and development of a complete 3 page web site. The second is a final report detailing the design and development of that Web site ~ Title Page and a minimum of 2 full pages or more. The Final Project Grade is worth 100points: The website itself is worth 75% of the final's total grade and the final report is worth 25%.

Web Site Requirements

For this part of the project, you are required to design and develop a complete Web site. The website we will be developing this semester will be for a fictional White water rafting business. The following are the specific requirements of the Web site (75pts):

  1. The Web site must consist of a home page and  2 child pages
  2. The Web site must be hosted on the web.
  3. Each page of the Web site must contain at least one image in addition to the logo and banner image.
  4. The main page of your Web site must satisfy the following requirements:
    1. Significant textual content consisting of appropriate content for your audience so that they can understand the meaning and message of this site
    2. Two section images that relate to the text in that section
    3. Working links to your two child pages
    4. Be laid out according to the weekly activities.
    5. Must have a logo image and a banner image
    6. Must have a complete footer with your info line and 3 social media icons with working links to each website
    7. Be visually appealing and easily readable
  5. Each child page of your Web site must satisfy the following requirements:
    1. Significant textual content consisting of appropriate content for your audience so that they can understand the meaning and message of each page.  Note: a contact us page must have more text content than the contact info only.  You must also have a few paragraphs about your company and an invitation to contact for more information.
    2. At least one image in addition to the banner and logo images
    3. Working links to your main page and your other child page
    4. Must have the complete header from the main page: logo image, navigation, and banner image
    5. Must have the same complete footer from the main page: name/copyright line and 3 social media icons with working links to each website
    6. Be visually appealing and easily readable

Final Report Requirements

For this part of the project, you are required to write a final report detailing the design and development of your completed Web site. The following is a description and an outline for the report: (use the provided template)

Title Page 
  • Separate Title page with your Title, your name and class, and the date.
Introduction
  • Introduce the subject of your website
  • Explain who the audience is for of your website
  • Include the URL for your completed website
Goals/Objectives
  • Explain what you hope to accomplish with this website
Design Process
  • Describe any major design changes you made to your website as you were completing the development process (i.e. does your website look and work the same as when you first envisioned it?)
Conclusion 
  • Describe what you learned from completing this project and contemplate how these skills may help you in the future.
