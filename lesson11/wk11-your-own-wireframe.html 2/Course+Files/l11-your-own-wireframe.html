Creating Your Own Wireframes

Activity Instructions

Estimated Time: 45 minutes

Read the entire activity before creating the wireframes

We now have a completed web page, but if you review the syllabus, you will remember that our final website should consist of 3 or more pages. We have more work to do!

If you refer back to our site plan, you will remember that there was a site map in there, and in fact we added a full navigation bar to our site with links to pages that currently don't exist. You need to choose 2 of those links that you will now design and make a page for (or if you have other ideas for related pages you could go with those as well). I have put together some Subpage Content on the next page appropriate for each of those pages that you are welcome to use as is or modify, or you can come up with your own content for the page.

The first step when designing is to put together a list of the elements that need to be on the page, then you can prioritize and start arranging those content elements together. This process of arranging is called wireframing. I provided the wireframe for the first page, you will need to create 2 more wireframes now...one for each page.

There are lots of good resources online about wireframing like:

  • Beginners guide to wireframing
  • Youtube - Creating Wireframes
  • I ♥ wireframes

There are a log of good tools on the internet to use for creating wireframes:

  • https://moqups.com/   (a great free site with loads of tools.  If you create a free account with your @byui.edu email and let me know, I can send in your info and get you a free education account for as long as you are a student ( or instructor)
  • cacoo.com
  • wireframe.cc 

If you do not  want to use one of these tools, you could try Powerpoint, Word, or Paint.   You essentially just need something that allows you to place boxes and text and move them around easily. 

Some designers still start first by sketching on paper for a rough draft...but for this assignment, what you turn in needs to be hi-tech.   If you use one of the online tools you can either export your wireframe as an image if it has that option or you could simply take a screenshot.

Note

To “take a screenshot” on a PC hit the “printscreen” button on your keyboard (Sometimes called “prt scr”, etc) Then you will need to paste it into something (Word for example) save as a .doc or better yet a .jpg or .png to submit.

To take a scene shot on a mac hit CMD-shift-4. Then drag around the area you want the screenshot of. It will create an image file on your desktop.

There are free apps out there that work to take screenshots.  Monosnap works great and allow you to use tools to write on the image, put arrows, etc

Step 01

Choose 2 of the subpages from the site map that you will complete this semester along with the home page.

Here are a copy of the content and image pages to use while creating your two wireframes:  

  • subpage content
  • images 1
  • images 2

Choose from the provided content and images or come up with your own so that you have a good idea for what needs to be on the page.

Using one of the tools above (I recommend moqup.com) to make a wireframe for your two sub-pages. If you have decided you would rather have different sub pages than you decided on in your site plan, no problem now is the time to change your mind.  Just change the two page names in your site plan and in your navigation list and move forward.

Each page must include the entire top of your page (from the <!DOCTYPE html> down to the end of the </header> and the entire footer down to the </html>.

You must have at least one image besides the logo and banner, enough text for the page to make sense and be able to stand on it’s own, and enough of the style elements from the home page that you can tell you are still on the same site (header, footer, navigation, font, colors, etc).

Since you will be using a hi-tech wireframe, you will use the actual content that you will have on the page in the wireframe. This will help you to get the size, shape, and placement of the text correct. For images you can use the actual images you will have on your page.   With the actual image, it is often easier to make decisions about size, shape, and placement than with a placeholder image (like you see on the original wireframe.  You can choose to use just a placeholder image if you wish, but like any works, it is always better to show a higher level of effort and attention to detail.
